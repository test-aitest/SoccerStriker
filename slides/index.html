A real-time, agent-driven football game
Why this app

Make AI agents that actually play football

  • The moment: the 2026 World Cup in the USA — football has the world's attention.
  • The idea: agents that genuinely play and cooperate, not scripted bots.
  • For everyone: natural motion control, like Nintendo Wii Sports — swing the iPhone to play.
What it is

4 vs 4. Both teams are autonomous AI agents.

  • Mac is the pitch — the full 3D match runs here.
  • iPhone is the controller — a motion controller in your hand.
  • You don't micromanage. You intervene at decisive moments by swinging your iPhone.
The hard problem

An LLM cannot drive a 60fps game

Calling a model every frame is impossible — network latency runs from hundreds of milliseconds to seconds, and the cost is prohibitive.

So how can a Gemini agent genuinely play in real time?

Strength 1 — Gemini as the brain

Hybrid agent loop

Gemini 3.5 Flash decides intentions every ~1.5s — per player: move / mark / pass / shoot / support
v
60Hz local engine executes those intentions every frame

Gemini is the decision-maker; the engine is the muscle. The game never stalls waiting on the model, and it stays in character.

Strength 2 — Believable team play

Real-time multi-agent behavior

  • Possession model, player roles, steering with separation (no clustering), man-marking, pass-lane checks, off-ball runs.
  • Pure-Swift, deterministic engine — covered by 16 unit tests.
  • Runs fully offline and gracefully falls back to rule AI if the API is unavailable.
Strength 3 — Human in the loop

Motion control, Wii-Sports style

  • iPhone motion controller: CoreMotion at 100Hz — swing to kick.
  • Connects to the Mac over Bonjour + UDP peer-to-peer — zero setup.
  • Decisive moments trigger timing and power gauges; nail it and the manager reacts: "tactics on point."
Where we put the effort

Craft that sells the fantasy

  • 3D pitch and players in Three.js (WebView) — run animation, uniforms per nation.
  • Nation select with real flags and player models; cut-in animations for shots, dribbles, and the manager.
  • Audio: procedural SFX plus stadium ambience. Gemini key handled via an .env-style xcconfig.
